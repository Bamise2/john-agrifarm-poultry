About Agrifarm Poultry

Six years of raising healthy birds in Mokola, Ibadan

Agrifarm Poultry started small in 2020 with a single pen of 50 broilers behind a family compound in Mokola. Today, we run a structured operation of 500 broilers per cycle alongside a steady laying flock — registered, vet-supervised, and built for the next stage of growth.

A registered, family-run farm

We operate as a duly registered business under the Corporate Affairs Commission (CAC), giving partners, suppliers and investors the assurance of a formally structured operation rather than an informal backyard setup.

CAC Registered — Business Name Reg. No. BN 7456231
📍 Farm Address: Off Mokola Road, Mokola, Ibadan, Oyo State

Our Story

Agrifarm Poultry was founded in 2020 by a small team determined to bring reliable, fresh poultry to families and businesses in Ibadan without the inconsistency common in the local market.

We started with 50 broilers and a single layer pen. Through disciplined reinvestment of profit — rather than external funding — we expanded housing, upgraded our feed program, and built direct relationships with over 1,200 customers, from households to hotels and event caterers.

Six years on, we're CAC-registered, running at 500-bird broiler capacity per cycle, and opening our doors to investors for the first time to fund our next phase of expansion.

Track record

What we've built so far

From one pen to a structured 500-bird operation — here's how the last six years unfolded.

2020

The first 50 broilers

Started in a single backyard pen in Mokola with 50 broilers, selling directly to neighbours and local market women.

2021

Layer flock introduced

Added our first 60 layer hens to begin daily egg supply alongside broiler sales, diversifying income.

2022

CAC registration

Formally registered Agrifarm Poultry as a business name with the Corporate Affairs Commission.

2023

New housing built

Constructed two additional deep-litter pens, raising broiler capacity from 150 to 350 birds per cycle.

2024

Hotel & restaurant partnerships

Began supplying three hotels and a chain of suya/restaurant outlets across Ibadan on standing weekly orders.

2025

500-bird capacity reached

Expanded to current 500-broiler cycle capacity and grew the layer flock to 180 hens.

2026

Opening to investors

Launching our first external investment round to fund a second farm site and double total capacity.

Common questions

Investor FAQ

How is my investment protected?

All funds are tied to specific stock and recorded against our CAC-registered business. Investors receive a written agreement and a report at the end of every cycle showing how funds were used and what was earned.

When do I receive returns?

Broiler cycles run 6–8 weeks, so Starter and Growth plan investors see their first profit share shortly after the relevant cycle closes and birds are sold.

Can I visit the farm before investing?

Yes — we encourage it. Reach out on WhatsApp and we'll schedule a walkthrough of the Mokola farm at a convenient time.

What happens if a cycle underperforms?

We share both upside and downside transparently in the cycle report. Our six-year track record has been consistently profitable, but agriculture carries normal seasonal and market risk like any farming business.
